Does anyone know what this blue line is called on this graph "Release"?

Milestones! Yes. Although no.

A milestone is used to indicate a distance from a paticular town

It is a way of trying to determine how much distance is left

Sprints are milestones. You know the frequency and you can look to see how quickly you are travelling towards the destination.

But when we say "Release" it now changes the nature - we are applying a state change on top of a measure of distance.

In Gantt charts milestones are rarly just moments in time - usually moments when you enter a meeting with a stakeholder to apologise for the late delivery, promising that the next quarter will, for some reason, be better (because all the hard stuff is done, apparently).

I also heard someone mention Deadlines.

What is a deadline? The term itself is thought by some to come from the US Civil War

It refers to a visual boundary around a prison. If a prisoner crossed it, they would be shot on site. We put these in our projects to indicate the same idea

whether the punishment for crossing your deadline is the same as that in the Civil War will largely depend on where you work.

And yet it's rarely such a clean death. Instead the project sponsor withdraws funding and asks for frequent updates, slowly starving the team of its resources until it either limps over the delivery line or dies trying at a much later date than anticipated.

In a world where deadlines cease to be a concern, is there much point in imposing artifical deadlines on a project?

Stereotypes

Bias amplification

In 2015, Amazon developed a recruitment tool designed to efficiently select top-tier job applicants from thousands, utilizing CV data.

This tool, trained with data from existing employees, inadvertently replicated organizational biases, with one model predicting candidate success based on gender.

Fortunately, Amazon discontinued the tool prior to integration into their recruitment process, a step not guaranteed from companies with differing moral standards.

All these language models have their own political bias.

It's not intentional, but it emreges from their own specific training set.

We need to be aware of them and ensure that we don't perpetuate them.
